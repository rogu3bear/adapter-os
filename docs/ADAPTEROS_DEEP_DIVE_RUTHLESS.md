## Executive summary
AdapterOS solves operational LoRA serving under deterministic and auditable constraints, not just adapter training. The control plane enforces a single inference path, policy hooks, replay metadata capture, and worker selection constraints. The worker executes over UDS and converts router outcomes into a canonical RouterRing before backend dispatch. Router behavior is determinism-aware, with Q15 gate quantization and stable tie-break ordering. Adapter lifecycle management is first-class through preload, integrity checks, hot-swap attach/swap/detach, and model/adaptor caching identities. Apple Silicon handling is explicit across Metal, CoreML, and MLX capability checks and backend fallback order. Training flow is orchestrated and phase-driven rather than a single monolithic script, and packaging targets the .aos format with signed content-addressed artifacts. Benchmark and load-test harnesses exist and are script-driven with documented output artifacts. Several asked claims are not present as explicit implementations, including hard latency SLAs and explicit global-vs-hierarchical router switch APIs. Those gaps are marked conservatively as Not found or Not implemented with evidence-of-absence entries.
